## Why this site exists

I could write in a journal and never show anyone, so why do I publish? Writing something and sending it out into the world is a form of closure. It's like taking a pile of half-baked ideas scribbled on sticky notes, and transforming it into a hefty paperback. What were once scattered thoughts is now a distinct point of view which stands on its own.

{{< draft >}}


 I'm not interested in publishing to sites like Medium, Wordpress, LinkedIn, and others in the same category—they all seem to have a profit incentive, constantly trying to upsell creators and users. I'd rather not get involved with that because this isn't about money. As a bonus I get full control over the appearance and content of my site.

I could write in a journal and never show anyone, so why do I publish? Deep down, I think we all have a desire to be heard out. If you've never met me, then my online presence is your only sample of what I'm like. It never feels good to have someone make assumptions about you, simply because they don't know you well. I publish in the hopes that a reader sees the world through my eyes. I hope they reply to my posts to share their perspective, so that I can understand them in return. I know there are people like me, out there—not necessarily people who agree with everything I say, but appreciators of candid feedback and diverse points of view.
